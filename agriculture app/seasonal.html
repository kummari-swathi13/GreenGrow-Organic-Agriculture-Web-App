Seasonal Crop Guide

Choosing the right crop for the right season improves yield, quality, and resistance to pests. Below is a detailed seasonal guide to help farmers plan efficiently throughout the year.

‚òÄÔ∏è Kharif Season (June to October)

Kharif crops are sown with the onset of monsoon and harvested at the end of the monsoon season.

  • Major Crops: Rice, Maize, Millets (Jowar, Bajra), Cotton, Soybean, Sugarcane
  • Climate: Warm and wet; requires good rainfall
  • Regions: Most parts of India (Punjab, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra)
  • Farming Tips: Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot, especially in paddy fields

‚ùÑÔ∏è Rabi Season (October to March)

Rabi crops are sown after the monsoon ends and harvested in spring.

  • Major Crops: Wheat, Barley, Mustard, Peas, Chickpeas (Gram)
  • Climate: Cool and dry; minimal rainfall required
  • Regions: Northern and central India (Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh)
  • Farming Tips: Requires timely irrigation and protection against early frost

üåº Zaid Season (March to June)

Zaid crops are grown between the Rabi and Kharif seasons during summer.

  • Major Crops: Watermelon, Muskmelon, Cucumber, Bitter Gourd, Fodder Crops
  • Climate: Hot and dry with controlled irrigation
  • Regions: Areas with irrigation facilities (Canals, Tubewells)
  • Farming Tips: Mulching and timely watering help retain moisture and prevent heat stress
